⛳ Why Thailand is a Golf Paradise

Thailand has become one of the world's top golf destinations, attracting players from around the globe with unbeatable value and excellent courses.

💰

Incredible Value

Play world-class courses for 50-75% less than similar quality in the US, UK, or Australia. A round at a top course costs what a cart fee might cost back home.

👧

Caddy Culture

Every course provides a personal caddy who reads greens, gives yardage, cleans clubs, and keeps you hydrated. Caddy fee: $8-15 plus tip.

☀️

Year-Round Play

Play 365 days a year. Even during rainy season (June-October), afternoon showers clear quickly and early morning rounds are usually perfect.

🏆

Championship Quality

Courses designed by Nicklaus, Faldo, Norman, and Schmidt-Curley. Many have hosted LPGA, Asian Tour, and international events.

🍽️

Clubhouse Experience

Excellent clubhouses with restaurants, pro shops, locker rooms, and spas. Many include lunch or snacks with green fees.

🎯

Variety of Courses

From mountain layouts in Chiang Mai to seaside links in Hua Hin to championship courses in Bangkok—every style available.

📍 Best Golf Destinations

With 10+ championship courses within 30 minutes, Hua Hin is purpose-built for golf retirement. Many retirees play 3-4 times per week here.

💡 Insider Tips for Golf in Thailand

🌅 Best Tee Times

Book 6:00-7:00 AM starts to beat the heat. Many courses finish rounds by noon. Afternoon rates are 30-50% cheaper but expect temperatures of 32-36°C.

👧 Caddy Etiquette

Standard tip is 300-500 THB ($9-15) per caddy. They read greens excellently (often better than your rangefinder). Treat them respectfully—they'll help your game.

📱 Booking

Book 3-7 days ahead for weekends at popular courses. Use golf booking sites for deals: GolfAsian, Golfscape, Thailand Golf Trips. Many expat groups negotiate group rates.

🌧️ Rainy Season

June-October brings afternoon showers. Morning rounds usually fine. Many courses offer "rain checks" if you can't finish. Green fees drop 20-40% off-season.

⚡ What's Included

Green fees typically include: cart (often), caddy (always), driving range balls (sometimes), towel & water (usually). Confirm inclusions when booking.

✈️ Bringing Clubs

Most airlines allow golf bags free or cheap to Thailand. Alternatively, rent quality clubs at courses ($20-40). Many expats buy good used sets locally.
