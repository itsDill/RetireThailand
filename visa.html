O-A Retirement Visa (Long Stay)

The Official Thai Government Retirement Visa

The O-A visa is the official retirement visa issued by Thai embassies and consulates abroad. It's designed specifically for retirees who want to spend their golden years in Thailand. This visa must be applied for from your home country before traveling.

üìã Requirements

  • Age 50 years or older at time of application
  • Passport valid for at least 18 months
  • Proof of 800,000 THB (~$22,500 USD) in bank account OR monthly income of 65,000 THB (~$1,825 USD)
  • Police clearance certificate from home country
  • Medical certificate (no prohibited diseases)
  • Health insurance with minimum $100,000 coverage (including $40,000 outpatient and $60,000 inpatient)
  • 4x6 cm passport photos (typically 3-5 photos)

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Official government visa with clear regulations
  • Lower upfront cost than Elite/LTR
  • Renewable indefinitely
  • Well-established process at most embassies
  • Full year stay from day one

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • Must apply from home country
  • Mandatory health insurance requirement
  • Police clearance needed
  • 90-day reporting required
  • Annual renewal at immigration
  • Need re-entry permit for travel

üìù Application Process

1

Gather Documents

Collect all required documents including police clearance, medical certificate, and bank statements. This can take 2-4 weeks.

2

Submit Application

Visit your nearest Thai embassy or consulate. Some require appointments. Processing time varies by location (1-10 business days).

3

Receive Visa

Once approved, you'll receive a 1-year O-A visa stamp in your passport. Must enter Thailand within 90 days of issuance.

4

Arrive in Thailand

Enter Thailand and your 1-year stay begins. Remember to get a re-entry permit if you plan to leave and return.

Non-Immigrant O Visa (Retirement)

The Flexible Alternative for Retirees

The Non-O visa is a more flexible option that can be obtained either from abroad or from within Thailand. Initial visa grants a 90-day stay, which can then be extended to a full year at a Thai immigration office. Many retirees prefer this route for its flexibility.

üìã Requirements

  • Age 50 years or older
  • Passport valid for at least 6 months
  • For extension: 800,000 THB in Thai bank account (must be deposited 2 months before application)
  • OR proof of 65,000 THB monthly income transferred to Thailand
  • OR combination of both totaling 800,000 THB
  • Proof of Thai address (TM.30 registration)
  • Photos and application forms

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Can apply from within Thailand
  • No health insurance requirement (for in-Thailand extensions)
  • No police clearance needed
  • Lower initial requirements
  • Can obtain at land borders or nearby countries
  • Good option if already in Thailand on tourist visa

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • Initial 90-day stay only
  • Must visit immigration for extension
  • Funds must be "seasoned" in Thai bank
  • 90-day reporting required
  • Re-entry permit needed for travel
  • Annual renewal process

üìù Extension Process (In Thailand)

1

Open Thai Bank Account

Deposit 800,000 THB. Most banks require a Non-O visa or tourist visa to open an account. Bangkok Bank is often the most foreigner-friendly.

2

Season Your Funds

Money must sit in the account for 2 months before applying. Don't withdraw below 800K or you may need to restart the seasoning period.

3

Gather Documents

Bank letter, bank book copies, passport copies, TM.30 receipt, photos, and completed application forms (TM.7).

4

Visit Immigration

Apply at your local immigration office. Processing is usually same-day. Extension fee is 1,900 THB.

Thailand Elite Visa

Premium Long-Term Stay with VIP Benefits

Thailand Elite is a membership program offering long-term visas from 5 to 20 years. It's perfect for those who want a hassle-free retirement without age restrictions or financial proof requirements. The program includes VIP airport services and eliminates the need for separate re-entry permits.

LTR Visa (Long-Term Resident)

For High-Income Retirees and Remote Workers

Launched in 2022, the LTR visa is Thailand's newest long-term visa option, specifically designed to attract wealthy retirees and remote workers. It offers significant advantages including work authorization and a reduced reporting requirement of once per year instead of every 90 days.

üìä LTR Visa Categories for Retirees

üèñÔ∏è Wealthy Pensioner

For retirees with stable pension or retirement income

  • Age: 50 years or older
  • Income: $80,000+ per year from pension/investments
  • Alternative: $40,000+ income + $250,000 in Thai property/bonds
  • Health Insurance: $50,000+ coverage required

üíª Work-from-Thailand

For digital nomads and remote workers

  • Age: No minimum
  • Income: $80,000+ per year (past 2 years)
  • Alternative: $40,000+ income + master's degree or IP
  • Employer: Company listed on stock exchange or with $150M+ revenue

‚úÖ Advantages

  • 10-year visa duration
  • Digital work permit included
  • Only annual reporting (not 90-day)
  • Flat 17% income tax rate
  • Unlimited re-entry
  • Fast-track airport services
  • Dependents eligible for same benefits
  • Report every 1 year instead of 90 days

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • High income requirements
  • Complex documentation
  • Must prove income for past 2 years
  • Health insurance mandatory
  • 50,000 THB application fee
  • Newer program with evolving rules

Essential Requirements & Tips

Key information every retiree should know

üìÑ Documentation Checklist

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity recommended)
  • Recent passport photos (4x6 cm)
  • Police clearance certificate (O-A only)
  • Medical certificate (O-A only)
  • Proof of finances (bank statements/income proof)
  • Health insurance documents (if required)
  • TM.30 address registration receipt
  • Completed application forms

üè¶ Financial Requirements

  • Option 1: 800,000 THB (~$22,500) in Thai bank
  • Option 2: 65,000 THB (~$1,825) monthly income
  • Option 3: Combination totaling 800,000 THB/year
  • Funds must be "seasoned" 2-3 months before extension
  • Maintain 400,000 THB for 3 months after extension
  • Embassy letters can verify income from abroad

üìç 90-Day Reporting

  • Required every 90 days for most visas
  • LTR visa only requires annual reporting
  • Online: Via immigration website (free)
  • In-person: At any immigration office (free)
  • By mail: Send registered letter
  • Report within 15 days before or 7 days after due date
  • Penalty: 2,000 THB fine for late reporting

üîÑ Re-entry Permits

  • Required for O-A and Non-O visas when leaving Thailand
  • Single re-entry: 1,000 THB
  • Multiple re-entry: 3,800 THB
  • Available at immigration offices and airports
  • Without permit, your visa is voided upon exit
  • Elite and LTR visas include unlimited re-entry

üè• Health Insurance

  • O-A visa: Mandatory ($100K coverage)
  • Non-O extension: Recommended but not required
  • LTR visa: Mandatory ($50K coverage)
  • Must cover inpatient and outpatient treatment
  • Some Thai insurers offer approved O-A plans
  • International insurance may be accepted

  • Withdrawing funds before the 3-month post-extension period
  • Missing 90-day reporting deadlines
  • Leaving Thailand without a re-entry permit
  • Not registering TM.30 within 24 hours of arrival
  • Applying for extension too late (must have 30+ days remaining)
  • Working without proper authorization

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common visa questions from retirees

Can I work on a retirement visa?

No. O-A and Non-O retirement visas do not permit any form of employment. This includes online work and freelancing. Only the LTR visa provides a digital work permit. Violation can result in fines, imprisonment, and deportation.

What happens if I can't maintain 800,000 THB?

If your bank balance drops below 400,000 THB in the 3 months after extension, or below 800,000 THB in the 2 months before your next extension, you may be denied renewal. Some people switch to Elite visa or income-based extensions as alternatives.

Can I buy property in Thailand?

Yes, visa status doesn't affect property rights. Foreigners can own condos (up to 49% of building's units) and lease land/houses for 30 years. Your retirement visa will make it easier to open bank accounts and complete transactions.

Is there a pathway to permanent residency?

Yes, after 3+ consecutive years on a Non-Immigrant visa, you can apply for permanent residency. Requirements include Thai language proficiency, income proof (80,000 THB/month), and fees (~200,000 THB). Only 100 PR permits are granted per country annually.

Which visa is best for couples?

If both partners are 50+, each can get individual retirement visas. If one is under 50, consider Elite Family Package or having the younger spouse get a Non-O visa based on marriage to the older spouse (if married to a Thai national is not applicable).

Can I switch between visa types?

Yes, you can transition between visa types. For example, you could start on tourist visa, convert to Non-O, then later upgrade to Elite or LTR. Each change requires a new application and may require leaving Thailand briefly.
