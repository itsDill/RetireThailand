Why Retire in Koh Chang?

Koh Chang, meaning "Elephant Island" for its shape, is Thailand's second-largest island and one of its best-kept secrets. Located in the eastern Gulf near the Cambodian border, it offers pristine beaches, dense jungle-covered mountains, and stunning waterfalls - all at a fraction of the cost of Phuket or Samui. It's perfect for nature lovers seeking an authentic, unspoiled island experience.

Koh Chang at a Glance

  • Thailand's second-largest island (after Phuket)
  • Part of a marine national park - protected natural beauty
  • Significantly cheaper than Phuket or Koh Samui
  • Dense jungle, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife
  • Less developed and more authentic Thai feel
  • Small but growing expat community
  • Easy day trips to Cambodia (visa runs)

üåßÔ∏è Rainy Season

Koh Chang has a pronounced rainy season from May to October. Many businesses close or reduce hours during this period. The west coast gets the heaviest rain. Some expats leave during monsoon season, while others enjoy the dramatic storms and lower prices.

Cost of Living in Koh Chang

Koh Chang is one of Thailand's most affordable islands. While prices are slightly higher than mainland due to transport costs, it's significantly cheaper than Phuket or Samui. Budget-conscious retirees can live very well here.

üí° Money-Saving Tips

  • Rent long-term for significant discounts (30-50% off)

  • Eat at local Thai restaurants away from the beaches
  • Shop at the local markets in Klong Prao and White Sand
  • Visit during low season (May-Oct) for better rental deals

Getting Around

You'll need your own transport on Koh Chang. The main road is hilly and winding. Options include scooter rental ($80-120/mo) or songthaew (shared pickup trucks) between beaches. The roads can be challenging during rainy season - experienced riders only.

Healthcare in Koh Chang

Healthcare options on Koh Chang are limited compared to larger islands. There's a government hospital and a few clinics, but for serious issues, you'll need to go to the mainland (Trat) or Bangkok.

üè• Koh Chang Hospital

Government hospital with basic facilities. Handles minor injuries, illnesses, and emergencies. Limited English but affordable. Best for non-serious issues.

Koh Chang is not ideal for retirees with serious or chronic health conditions requiring regular specialist care. The nearest quality hospitals are in Trat (mainland) or Pattaya/Bangkok (4-5 hours). Make sure you have comprehensive health insurance with evacuation coverage.

Healthcare Costs

  • Basic dental: $20-40
