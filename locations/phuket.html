Why Retire in Phuket?

Phuket is Thailand's largest island and a world-famous beach destination. It offers an upscale island lifestyle with international restaurants, beach clubs, and a large expat population. Perfect for those who want beach living with all modern conveniences and don't mind paying a bit more for paradise.

Phuket at a Glance

  • Thailand's largest island with 30+ stunning beaches
  • International airport with direct flights to Europe, Australia, and Asia
  • Good hospitals including Bangkok Hospital Phuket
  • Large, established expat community with active social scene
  • World-class restaurants, beach clubs, and nightlife
  • Easy island hopping to Phi Phi, James Bond Island, and more

Cost of Living in Phuket

Phuket is more expensive than mainland Thailand due to its popularity as a tourist destination. However, if you live away from tourist areas, costs become much more reasonable.

üí° Money-Saving Tips

  • Live in Rawai or Chalong instead of Patong for 30-40% lower rent
  • Rent a scooter ($100/mo) - essential for getting around the island
  • Shop at local markets like Banzaan Fresh Market instead of tourist areas
  • Join beach clubs for day passes instead of expensive hotel beaches

Best Areas for Retirees

Phuket has distinct areas with very different vibes. Most retirees avoid the tourist party areas and settle in quieter parts of the island:

The southern tip of Phuket is the expat favorite. Rawai has a laid-back fishing village feel with excellent seafood restaurants. Nai Harn has one of Phuket's most beautiful beaches.

Healthcare Costs

Healthcare costs in Phuket are similar to Bangkok:

  • Doctor visit: $30-50
  • Dental cleaning: $25-40
  • MRI scan: $200-400
  • Health insurance: $150-400/month (depending on age)
