Why Retire in Koh Samui?

Koh Samui is Thailand's second-largest island and a dream destination for those seeking a tropical island retirement. Known for its coconut palm-fringed beaches, crystal-clear waters, and thriving wellness scene, Samui offers an upscale island lifestyle that's more developed and accessible than neighboring Koh Phangan or Koh Tao.

Koh Samui at a Glance

  • Stunning white-sand beaches and turquoise water
  • International airport with direct flights to Bangkok
  • Well-developed infrastructure for an island
  • World-class spas and wellness retreats
  • Good private hospital on the island
  • Mixed international expat community
  • Laid-back tropical lifestyle

üåßÔ∏è Monsoon Season

Koh Samui's monsoon (October-December) is different from the rest of Thailand. The east coast gets heavy rain, while the west coast is relatively sheltered. Many expats travel during this period, though some prefer the quieter, greener island.

Cost of Living in Koh Samui

Koh Samui is one of the more expensive places to live in Thailand. Island life means higher prices for groceries, fuel, and imported goods. However, it's still affordable compared to Western beach destinations.

üí° Island Premium

Expect to pay 15-30% more for groceries and everyday items compared to the mainland. The duty-free airport doesn't fully offset this. Many long-term residents stock up on certain items when visiting the mainland or Bangkok.

Island Transport

You'll need your own transport on Samui - there's no public transport system. Options include scooter rental ($100-150/mo), car rental ($400-600/mo), or buying a used vehicle. Many retirees buy a scooter for daily use and rent a car for trips.

Healthcare in Koh Samui

Koh Samui has good healthcare for an island, with Bangkok Hospital Samui being the main facility. For complex procedures, many people fly to Bangkok (1 hour).

üè• Bangkok Hospital Samui

Part of the Bangkok Hospital network with good facilities and English-speaking staff. Full-service hospital that handles most needs. JCI accredited.

Healthcare Costs

Healthcare on Samui is slightly more expensive than mainland Thailand:

  • Doctor visit: $35-60
  • Dental cleaning: $30-50
  • Health checkup: $120-300
  • Health insurance: $200-500/month (depending on age)

üí° Emergency Evacuation

For serious emergencies, medical evacuation to Bangkok is available. Make sure your health insurance covers emergency evacuation. Bangkok Hospital Samui coordinates transfers to Bangkok hospitals when needed.
