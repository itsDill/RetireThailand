Why Retire in Hua Hin?

Hua Hin is a charming seaside town that's been a royal retreat since the 1920s when King Rama VII built his summer palace here. It's popular with retirees who want a relaxed beach lifestyle without the party scene. World-class golf courses, good hospitals, and easy access to Bangkok make it ideal for active, mature retirees.

Hua Hin at a Glance

  • Royal connections - home to the King's summer palace
  • 15+ championship golf courses within 30 minutes
  • Just 2.5-3 hours from Bangkok by car
  • Strong Scandinavian and European expat community
  • Family-friendly with no red-light district
  • Good local hospital (Bangkok Hospital Hua Hin)
  • Less touristy than Phuket or Pattaya

Cost of Living in Hua Hin

Hua Hin is moderately priced - more affordable than Phuket but slightly higher than Chiang Mai. It offers excellent value for beach living with golf access.

💡 Golf Tip

Green fees at Hua Hin courses range from $30-80 for locals/expats. Many courses offer membership packages for regular players that can save significant money. Twilight rates (after 2pm) are often 40-50% cheaper.

Best Areas for Retirees

Hua Hin is relatively compact compared to other Thai destinations. Here are the main areas where expats live:

Hua Hin Center

Town Center

Close to the beach, night market, restaurants, and the famous Hua Hin train station. Walkable and convenient, though can be busy during holidays.

💰 Rent: $400-800/mo 🚶 Walkable: Yes

Khao Takiab

South of Center

Quieter area south of town near Chopsticks Hill with its hilltop temple. More local feel, good restaurants, and a nice beach. Popular with long-term expats.

💰 Rent: $350-650/mo 🏖️ Beach: Good

Black Mountain

Golf Community

Purpose-built area around the Black Mountain Golf Course. Popular with golfers wanting to live near the fairways. Mix of villas and condos.

💰 Rent: $600-1,500/mo ⛳ Golf: On-site

Cha-Am

North of Hua Hin

Separate beach town 25km north of Hua Hin. More local Thai feel, significantly cheaper, and good beach. Popular with budget-conscious retirees.

💰 Rent: $250-450/mo 💵 Budget: Friendly

Pranburi

South of Hua Hin

Very quiet area 30km south of Hua Hin near Sam Roi Yot National Park. Beautiful nature, secluded beaches, and very peaceful. Best for those who really want to escape.

💰 Rent: $300-600/mo 🌿 Nature: Excellent

Soi 88 / Soi 94 Area

Expat Corridor

Long sois running from the main road to the beach, lined with condos, restaurants, and shops. Very popular with expats and has a community feel.

💰 Rent: $400-750/mo 👥 Expats: Many

Healthcare in Hua Hin

Hua Hin has good healthcare for a town its size, with Bangkok Hospital Hua Hin being the standout facility. For complex procedures, Bangkok is only 2.5-3 hours away.

🏥 Bangkok Hospital Hua Hin

Part of the Bangkok Hospital network with good facilities and English-speaking staff. Handles most medical needs and has an emergency department.

📍 Phetkasem Road

🏥 San Paulo Hua Hin Hospital

Private hospital with reasonable prices. Good for routine care and minor procedures. Less expensive than Bangkok Hospital.

📍 Hua Hin Center

🏥 Hua Hin Hospital

Government hospital with very low prices. Can be crowded and have limited English, but adequate for basic care.

📍 Phetkasem Road

Healthcare Costs

Healthcare in Hua Hin is similar to other provincial areas:

  • Doctor visit: $25-40
  • Dental cleaning: $20-35
  • Health checkup: $80-150
  • Health insurance: $130-350/month (depending on age)

💡 Bangkok Access

For specialized care, Bumrungrad and other Bangkok hospitals are just 2.5-3 hours away by car or 4 hours by train. Some expats maintain relationships with Bangkok doctors for annual checkups.

Pros & Cons of Retiring in Hua Hin

✅ Pros

  • Relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere
  • World-class golf courses (15+)
  • Easy access to Bangkok (2.5-3 hours)
  • Less touristy than islands
  • Good local hospital
  • Strong European expat community
  • Nice beach and seafood restaurants
  • Night market and local culture

⚠️ Cons

  • Beach not as nice as islands (greyish sand)
  • Limited nightlife and entertainment
  • Can feel quiet or boring
  • Need car/scooter to get around
  • No international airport (use Bangkok)
  • Fewer dining options than larger cities
  • Can get crowded on Thai holidays
