Why Retire in Koh Sichang?

Koh Sichang is Thailand's best-kept retirement secret. This tiny island in the Gulf of Thailand, just 12 kilometers off the coast of Si Racha in Chonburi province, offers something increasingly rare: authentic Thai island life without a single tourist resort in sight. At roughly 7 square kilometers, it's small enough to feel intimate but large enough to never feel claustrophobic.

Koh Sichang at a Glance

  • Ultra-affordable island living ($800-1,200/month total)
  • Only 1.5 hours from Bangkok (ferry + drive)
  • Virtually zero Western tourists
  • 100% authentic Thai community life
  • Royal palace ruins and historic temples
  • Fresh seafood daily at local prices
  • No motorbike taxis - peaceful tuk-tuks instead
  • Close to Si Racha for shopping and healthcare

⚠️ Important Considerations

Koh Sichang is NOT for everyone. There are no Western supermarkets, no expat community, and very limited English spoken. You'll need to learn basic Thai and embrace true local living. The nearest hospital is on the mainland (30-minute ferry). This is for adventurous retirees who want to fully immerse in Thai culture.

Getting to Koh Sichang

Take a bus from Bangkok's Eastern Bus Terminal (Ekkamai) to Si Racha (1 hour, ~100 baht), then a short tuk-tuk to the pier. Ferries depart hourly and cost 50 baht for the 45-minute journey. Total travel time: approximately 2-2.5 hours door-to-door from central Bangkok.

Cost of Living in Koh Sichang

Koh Sichang offers some of the lowest living costs in Thailand - even cheaper than Chiang Mai in many respects. Because there's no tourist economy, prices remain at true local Thai levels. The trade-off is limited Western comforts and amenities.

💡 Food is Incredibly Cheap

Local restaurants serve rice dishes for 40-60 baht ($1.20-1.80). Fresh seafood caught that morning costs a fraction of tourist area prices. A whole grilled fish with rice and vegetables runs about 100-150 baht ($3-4.50). Many retirees eat out for every meal and still spend under $200/month on food.

💡 Housing Reality

There's no rental market aimed at foreigners. You'll need to find housing through local connections or by visiting and asking around. Options range from simple rooms in local homes to small standalone houses. Long-term rates are highly negotiable. Some retirees have found entire houses for 5,000-8,000 baht/month ($150-240).

Areas of Koh Sichang

Koh Sichang is small enough that you can walk or cycle anywhere in 15-20 minutes. However, the island does have distinct areas with different characters:

Tha Bon (Main Village)

Most Convenient

The main pier area and commercial center. Here you'll find the market, restaurants, 7-Eleven, small shops, and the health center. Most practical for daily living with everything within walking distance.

💰 Rent: $180-400/mo 🏪 Amenities: Best

Tha Lang (West Side)

Quieter Village Life

A small fishing village on the western coast. More local, more peaceful, with waterfront views. Has its own small pier and a few local restaurants. Sunset views over the mainland.

💰 Rent: $150-300/mo 🎣 Vibe: Fishing village

Chakrabongse Hill Area

Historic & Scenic

The elevated area near the former royal palace ruins. Cooler temperatures, sea breezes, and panoramic island views. Less housing available but beautiful setting. Near Wat Atsadang Nimit temple.

💰 Rent: $200-450/mo 🏛️ Character: Historic

Tham Phang Beach Area

Beach Living

Near the island's only real beach (rocky but swimmable). More isolated from the village but beautiful natural setting. A few guesthouses and restaurants. Good for those who prioritize nature over convenience.

💰 Rent: $200-400/mo 🏖️ Beach: Yes

Island Transport

Forget cars - the island has narrow roads perfect for motorcycles and bicycles. Most locals use scooters. You can rent a motorbike for about 200-300 baht/day or buy a used one for 15,000-25,000 baht. Tuk-tuks (motorcycle sidecars) are available for trips around the island at fixed rates. Many retirees simply walk or cycle everywhere.

Healthcare in Koh Sichang

Healthcare is Koh Sichang's biggest limitation. The island has a small health center for basic care, but anything serious requires a trip to the mainland. This is a critical consideration for retirees with ongoing health needs.

🏥 Koh Sichang Health Promoting Hospital

Small government health center on the island. Can handle minor issues, basic medications, and first aid. Staff are helpful but limited equipment and expertise. Open during business hours.

📍 Tha Bon village

🏥 Si Racha Hospital

The nearest full-service hospital, located on the mainland in Si Racha. Government hospital with good facilities. 30-minute ferry + 10-minute drive. Handles most medical needs.

📍 Si Racha (Mainland)

🏥 Samitivej Sriracha Hospital

Private international-standard hospital in Si Racha. English-speaking doctors, modern equipment, higher prices. Excellent for routine checkups and non-emergency procedures.

📍 Si Racha (Mainland)

🏥 Bangkok Hospital Pattaya

For specialized care, the international-standard Bangkok Hospital Pattaya is about 1 hour from Si Racha. JCI accredited with full range of specialties and English-speaking staff.

📍 Pattaya (45 min from Si Racha)

💡 Healthcare Strategy

Successful Koh Sichang retirees typically: (1) Choose comprehensive health insurance that covers evacuation, (2) Schedule regular checkups at Si Racha hospitals during mainland trips, (3) Keep a supply of any necessary medications on hand, and (4) Have a plan for reaching the mainland quickly in emergencies. The last ferry is at 8 PM, but emergency boats can be arranged.

🚨 Emergency Considerations

Be realistic about emergency response times. While emergency evacuation is possible, you're still on an island with limited medical resources. Retirees with serious chronic conditions should consider whether Koh Sichang is appropriate for their situation. The mainland is always just a ferry ride away, but that ferry ride exists.

Daily Life on Koh Sichang

Living on Koh Sichang means embracing a radically simpler lifestyle. There's no nightlife, no shopping malls, no Western restaurants, and very few other foreigners. What you get instead is something increasingly rare: authentic, unhurried Thai island life.

Morning Routine

Daily Life

Wake to roosters and temple bells. Buy fresh coffee from a local vendor (20 baht). Walk to the morning market for fruits, vegetables, and fresh seafood. Watch fishing boats return with the day's catch.

Activities & Recreation

Things to Do

Explore the royal palace ruins. Visit Chinese temples. Swim at Tham Phang Beach. Cycle around the island. Fish from the rocks. Read books. Learn Thai. Make friends with locals. Embrace slow living.

Social Life

Community

Your social circle will be Thai locals. Learning basic Thai is essential and rewarding. Locals are curious and welcoming. You'll become a known figure in the community - the friendly foreigner who chose their island.

Regular Mainland Trips

Practicalities

Most retirees visit Si Racha weekly or bi-weekly for shopping, banking, and healthcare. Stock up on anything you can't find on the island. Some keep a small apartment on the mainland for extended stays.

Internet & Connectivity

Mobile data coverage is good (AIS, True Move, DTAC all work). Many homes can get fiber internet installed. 4G speeds are sufficient for video calls and streaming. Work remotely? It's possible, though power outages occasionally occur.

💡 Is Koh Sichang Right For You?

Yes if: You want authentic Thai immersion, are comfortable without Western comforts, have basic Thai language skills (or willingness to learn), are in good health, and crave simplicity and peace.

No if: You need regular specialized healthcare, want an expat social circle, require Western amenities, or need to be constantly entertained. Consider Hua Hin or Chiang Mai instead.

Try Before You Commit

Before making Koh Sichang your home, spend at least 2-4 weeks there. Several simple guesthouses exist for short-term stays:

Where to Stay While Exploring

  • Sichang Palace: The nicest option, around 800-1,500 baht/night
  • Charlie's Bungalows: Basic but clean, 400-600 baht/night
  • Local guesthouses: Around the pier area, 300-500 baht/night
  • Monthly rooms: Negotiate directly, 4,000-8,000 baht/month

Reconnaissance Trip Tips

Spend weekdays AND weekends (weekends bring Thai day-trippers). Walk every part of the island. Eat at local restaurants. Practice your Thai. Talk to the few long-term farang who live there. Check the morning and evening ferry schedules. Imagine living here full-time - can you see yourself?
